Blond

A few years ago, back when I was still in highschool, I became greatly interested in all the culture surrounding graffiti, sticker and street art. I just loved the idea of being able to leave your artistic mark in the streets wherever I went, in Madrid or in any place I visit, and making it accesible for everyone to see it. I then decided my own alias, Blond, and started drawing characters and signatures with my name on stickers and pasting them all around Madrid. I was captivated by that concept of making my drawings become famous, but in a certain anonymous way.

As time passed, my style evolved and I realised that this sort of personal project could transcend sticker art. I already had two characters that I always used for my stickers: Blond (the yellow one, based of a character I drew back when I used to make webcomics) and Pez (the green, lake monster like creature), but I wanted to go further. I started making designs with sentimental, philosophical or simply cool messages that represented my ideologies, values or simply the things I like. In addition, I also took interest in drawing cultural elements from my surroundings, such as how young people as me like to see our reality or the frequent spots in Madrid we all go to.

From that moment on, I haven't stopped trying new things under my alias. I have designed logos, sold my art, designed clothes, drawn music covers or even directed, animated and edited videoclips. All of this, mantaining my own artistic style - a mixture of cartoon and graffiti art, which I've found to be really attractive to most people of my age. Blond has given me the opportunity to meet lots of new people, from tattoo artists to graphic designers, from whom I constantly learn new things and who inspire me on my every day creative journey.

Overall, I think Blond is that little part of me that lets me keep drawing as a hobby rather than an obligation. It grants me the freedom to express myself artistically without anyone telling me what or how to draw. With this alias, I am able to connect with the mentalities of the people that surround me, share common interests and grow as a person through this colorful designs that, even though can be seen as simple, plain or uncomplicated, they usually have powerful intentions and messages for young people.

I mostly do all the art using Adobe Photoshop, but I've also used vector art software, such as Adobe Illustrator or Inkscape, specially for animations and logo designs. If you wish to see some of those animations, please head back to the Portfolio sections and search for "2D Animations".

I've always felt that Blond is heavily connected with my music taste and my personal experiences, since most of the design ideas come from the inspiration these aspects of my life bring me. I believe it is an interesting way to make people get to now me, without really knowing who is the person behind Blond.

Lucas Rubio.
