Upstream

Project Date Sept. 2023 - Dic. 2023
Role

Art Director, Scrum Master, GUI Design and Implementation

Upstream is a WebGL game developed in Unity by myself and some of my university colleagues for our Web Games class. Designed to be enjoyable for diverse audiences, our primary objective was to create a game that not only entertained but also served as a tool for assessing computational thinking in children aged 5 to 12.

Featuring 14 distinct levels, players control Darwin, a small salamander-like creature who, separated from his mother as an egg, must conquer his fears and swim upstream to reunite with his family. The game unfolds Darwin's evolution from tadpole to adult salamander across three unique environments, each introducing new mechanics and captivating elements.

This was the first project in which my colleagues and I were able to organize ourselves and act as a proper game studio. Each one of us could work on different aspects of the game: while I designed the visual aspects and concept art, others worked on implementing game mechanics or made the 3D models for the levels. I really enjoyed working with them, as they were not only my colleagues but also my friends, and the final result made us realise that developing games is not only a dream but our current reality.

Apart from the game, we were also guided by our teachers to work on social media marketing and the creation of a portfolio for our fictional game studio, Tapeo Games. We learned that this is a really important aspect for game development that we should always take into account. Upstream could be improved in several different ways, but we are really proud of the final result.
